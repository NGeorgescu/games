Fifteen

Take turns claiming numbers 1–9 (each can be taken once). First to hold any three numbers that sum to 15 wins. Sounds like arithmetic — but it is tic-tac-toe in disguise. Spot the fifteens yourself, then flip on the magic square to see why.

How it works — the secret

Write the numbers 1–9 into a 3×3 magic square — the ancient Lo Shu arrangement:

8   1   6
3   5   7
4   9   2

Every row, column and long diagonal adds to 15. There are exactly eight ways to pick three of the numbers 1–9 that total 15 — and those eight triples are precisely the eight lines of the square (3 rows + 3 columns + 2 diagonals).

  1. Claiming a number = marking its cell in the square.
  2. Holding three numbers that sum to 15 = holding a full line — three in a row.
  3. So "pick numbers summing to 15" is tic-tac-toe. Same game, same perfect strategy: with best play by both sides it is always a draw.

The computer here plays the position as tic-tac-toe on this square. On Perfect it runs a full minimax search, so it never loses and punishes any slip. On Easy it moves at random, so a learner can win — and, more usefully, can practise seeing the fifteens without a grid. The game is also known as Number Scrabble, Pick15 or 3-to-15.
