Tiny House Chess

A pocket chess variant on a 4×4 board with four fairy pieces. Capture an enemy piece and it flips to your hand — on a later turn you may drop it back onto any empty square instead of moving (crazyhouse rules). Checkmate the enemy King to win.

Stalemate rule: if it's your turn and you have no legal move but you're not in check, you win. So you can never win by stalemating your opponent — you must always leave them a move, or you lose. (Being checkmated — no move while in check — is still a loss.)

The start is a 180° rotation: White's back rank a1→d1 is King · Wazir · Dragonknight · Ferz with a pawn on a2; Black is the same shape rotated, so the kings sit on diagonally opposite corners.

How the pieces move

Crazyhouse drops: capturing a piece adds it to your hand (a promoted piece reverts to a pawn). On your turn you may drop a hand piece onto any empty square — pawns included, even on your own home rank. The only restriction: a pawn can't be dropped on its promotion rank (the far rank). A drop may give check or mate.
